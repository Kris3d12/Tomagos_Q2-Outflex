The Idlers and the Fools

from the Book of Proverbs

You idler go to the ant,
Watch her ways and be wise

She secures food in the summer
she has no master, no steward or overseer,
And stores up provisions during harvest time

Your laziness creeps upon you
And then, like a tramp, poverty comes
Bringing misery like a vagabond.

How long, you idler, are you going to lie around? When
do you intend to rise up from the sleep? A little sleep,
a little drowsiness.
